TRUE

"Screen time before bed can affect sleep quality"

Technology • Verified 6 hours ago • Expert: Dr. Michael Rodriguez, Sleep Specialist

This claim is scientifically accurate. Blue light from screens can suppress melatonin production, making it harder to fall asleep. Studies recommend avoiding screens 1-2 hours before bedtime for better sleep hygiene.

Sources:

  • Sleep Foundation - Blue Light Research
  • Harvard Health - Sleep and Technology
  • National Sleep Institute Studies
